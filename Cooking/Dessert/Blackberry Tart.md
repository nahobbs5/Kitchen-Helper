🧁 Mini Blackberry Swirl Tartlets (Allergy-Free)
Free from: gluten, dairy, eggs, oats, nuts
Makes: 12–14 mini tartlets (standard muffin tin)
Each tartlet ends up with ~1 teaspoon blackberry or less.
## 🥧 Crust (Press-In, No Rolling)
Ingredients
- 1½ cups gluten-free all-purpose flour (no oat flour)
- ⅓ cup sugar
- ¼ tsp salt
- ⅓ cup neutral oil (canola or avocado)
- 3–4 tbsp cold water
### Instructions
1. Mix flour, sugar, salt
2. Stir in oil, then water until dough holds together
3. Press ~2 tbsp dough into each muffin cup - Bottom + gently up the sides
4. Bake at 350°F for 12–15 min, until just set
5. Cool slightly in pan
## 🍮 Coconut Vanilla Custard Filling
### Ingredients
- 1½ cups full-fat coconut milk
- ⅓ cup sugar
- 3 tbsp cornstarch
- 1½ tsp vanilla
- Pinch salt
### Instructions
1. Whisk everything in a saucepan
2. Cook over medium heat, whisking constantly
3. When thick like pudding, remove from heat
4. Let cool 5 minutes (still spoonable)
## Blackberry Swirl (Low-Exposure)
### Ingredients
- ½ cup blackberries (fresh or frozen)
- 1–2 tbsp sugar
- Splash of water
### Instructions
1. Simmer berries + sugar + water 5–7 minutes
2. Mash thoroughly
3. Strain out seeds (important)
4. Cool slightly

## Assemble
1. Spoon custard into crusts (almost to the top)
2. Add ¼–½ tsp blackberry sauce per tartlet
3. Swirl gently with a toothpick
4. Chill 2–3 hours until fully set
## Serve & Store
1. Remove gently with a butter knife
2. Serve chilled


Keeps 3 days refrigerated
Can be made a day ahead
