## Ingredients
- 4 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 tbsp sugar (optional)
- 1 tsp baking soda
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp caraway seeds (optional)
- 1 cup raisins (optional)
- 1¾ cups buttermilk
Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (218°C).
2. In a large bowl, whisk together:
	- flour
	- sugar
	- baking soda
	- salt
3. Stir in caraway seeds and raisins if using.
4. Add buttermilk and mix just until a shaggy dough forms.
5. Turn dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead gently 8–10 times (just enough to bring it together).
6. Shape into a round loaf about 1½–2 inches thick.
7. Place on a baking sheet or cast-iron skillet.
8. Cut a deep cross on top with a sharp knife.
9. Bake 30–40 minutes until golden and hollow-sounding when tapped.
## Tips
- The cross helps the center cook and is also an old Irish tradition.
- Best eaten warm with butter.
- If you don’t have buttermilk:
	Mix 1¾ cups milk + 1½ tbsp lemon juice or vinegar and let sit 5 minutes.
### Easy Variation
Brush the loaf with melted butter when it comes out of the oven.
